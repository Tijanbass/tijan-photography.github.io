MY FIRST WEEK LEARNING ADOBE PHOTOSHOP EXPERIENCE.

First Day Experience Using Adobe Photoshop .


When I was in middle school, I always wondered how some of the context-based animated and graphic images and photos were.


Until I saw my friend Muhammed display an interface of different designing software (Photoshop, Illustrator, and CorelDRAW), amazing but confusing . Since then, I have had the spirit of starting to learn how to use one of those designing software, but I was not sure about which one to start with. Another thing that delayed me from starting is that I kept procrastinating. I wish I had started way earlier. Before my departure to Japan, I decided to install one of the design software packages, “Photoshop,” based on the video recommendation from YouTube and started practising in the first week of April upon my arrival.


What Were Your First Impressions?


In the past, I had used Paint for designing, and I thought the interface would be similar, but I was disappointed—not really disappointed, but surprisingly amazed with the software. Everything seemed complicated before I realized it was just a combination of different basic designing tools.


How Do You Begin to Learn?

As the saying goes, practice makes perfect, and I felt compelled to take the same approach to the learning process by practising. I decided to open YouTube and follow a beginner’s guide step by step. Initially, it was difficult because I had to spend a long time trying to locate certain tools. Now, I am starting to understand how to use some of the basic tools.
